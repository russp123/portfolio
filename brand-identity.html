Brand Identity System

Logo & Visual Design

Glamporise is Kingdomes' luxury glamping sister brand — built on the idea that luxury shouldn't mean leaving nature behind. I designed its visual identity from scratch: 25+ exploratory logo concepts narrowed to one final mark, a five-colour palette, a two-font type system, and a 12-page brand guidelines document that keeps every future touchpoint consistent. The identity now runs across the live website, funnel pages, ebooks, and social content.

Concept Development

The mark didn't start as a flower. Early explorations ranged from literal waratah illustrations and royal crests (a nod to the Kingdomes parent brand) to geometric florals, leaf studies, and campfire flames — 25+ directions in total. The final mark emerged where three of those threads crossed: petals, crown, and fire in a single shape.

One Mark, Three Stories

Every element of the final logo carries a meaning, documented in the brand guidelines so the story travels with the mark:

The Waratah Petals

Drawn from Australia's iconic waratah flower — heritage, and a commitment to natural luxury.

The Crown

A royal silhouette connecting Glamporise to Kingdomes — the evolution of luxury glamping.

The Campfire Wood

The logs at the base — the core element of every camping experience.

The Complete Flame

Together the three parts form a campfire with rising flames: nature, luxury, and the timeless gathering around fire.

The Logo System

The final suite covers every context the brand publishes in: the full horizontal lockup in brand green, black for print and light surfaces, and white for photography and dark surfaces — plus stacked and icon-only variants, each with documented clear-space and usage rules.

Colour

Deep Sage Emerald — drawn from the waratah's foliage — leads the palette, softened by Soft Sand in place of stark white, anchored by black and white for contrast, with Golden Yellow reserved as a sparing accent for highlights and calls-to-action.

Typography

Two voices: Cartesius, the elegant serif carrying the wordmark, and Poppins, the geometric sans doing the everyday work — headlines in bold, body text in regular, with documented sizes and spacing so hierarchy stays consistent no matter who's designing.

The Guidelines

Everything above ships as a 12-page brand guidelines document — brand story, logo anatomy and variations, dos and don'ts, colour usage rules, and typography specs — so anyone touching the brand can keep it consistent without asking. Its closing note sets the tone for how it's meant to be used:

These guidelines exist to keep Glamporise consistent and recognizable. But they're not prison rules. If something isn't covered here and you're unsure, ask yourself: does this feel natural, luxurious, and honest?
