Email Campaign Design

A four-email launch sequence designed for Kingdomes' Aura Dome — Australia's first frameless geodesic dome — walking a subscriber from first reveal to a booked call. Each email is a scrolling story in the brand's own visual language: full-bleed photography, polaroid-style collages, a gold-and-white palette, and one clear pair of calls to action. The sequence leans on honest scarcity (50 units made in 2026, 10 trial slots) rather than hype, and every send rides the brand's authenticated Mailgun infrastructure so the design lands in Primary inboxes and renders cleanly across Gmail, Outlook, and mobile.

01 · The Reveal

The launch announcement — "We've been building geodesic domes for years… We built it." A product story that walks from credibility to the frameless reveal, closing on scarcity (only 50 being made in 2026) and two paths: see the product, or book a 15-minute call.

02 · The Trial Offer

"See it first." — a risk-reversal offer for buyers on the fence about a $12,000–$19,000 purchase: spend one night in the Aura Dome at Bilpin for $300, and if you buy within 30 days the full $300 credits toward your deposit. Either way, you'll know.

03 · The Delivery Update

A manufacturing update that turns logistics into urgency: 50 units in 2026 because each dome is made to order, with priority delivery (March–June) versus standard (July–December) — so buyers who want winter season know allocations are filling.

04 · The Last Call

The follow-up nudge — two honest countdowns in one email: only 10 Bilpin trial slots left, and priority delivery nearly allocated. Closes with a concrete agenda for the 15-minute call, so "book a call" feels like a plan rather than a pitch.
