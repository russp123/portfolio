Ebook Design

Glamporise needed a paid front door for its Stage 1 membership — something that earns its AU$27 price tag before a customer ever books a call. I designed a 34-page ebook that does that: original illustrated cover artwork, a custom typography system built to carry a lot of dense, practical advice without ever feeling like a wall of text, and consistent information design across every page. It's the flagship piece in a broader content set I also designed — a 15-page masterclass guide and a 3-part founding-offer guide series, 50+ pages total.

The Cover

The cover carries the whole premise in one image: a geodesic dome glowing at dusk, set inside a silhouette of the NSW state outline, framed by tree line and a topographic contour pattern along the bottom edge. Warm gold against near-black — the same palette Glamporise's brand identity runs on — so the product reads as premium before anyone opens the first page.

The System

Thirty-four pages of practical, sometimes blunt advice needed a layout that could hold attention without getting monotonous. Every chapter opens with a large numbered marker and a punchy headline, body copy stays short and scannable, and recurring "Pro Tip" callouts and pull-quotes break up the page rhythm — all built on the color-weighted typography system I set up so headings, emphasis, and asides stay visually distinct without a redesign per page. Real property photography sits alongside the text rather than as generic stock filler.

Part of a Larger Set

The Decoder doesn't stand alone — it's the anchor of a full content library I designed for the membership, including a companion guide on the council approval process ("Most Dome Projects," pictured) and the rest of the founding-offer series. Same typography system, same illustration language, same gold-and-charcoal palette carried across every piece, so the whole collection reads as one product line rather than a stack of unrelated PDFs.
