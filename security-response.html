Security Incident Response

In March 2026, kingdomes.com.au came under a coordinated attack. A volumetric DDoS — 867,000 requests from a concentrated set of foreign IP ranges — hit the site at the same time as a deeper infiltration: attackers had already planted four backdoor admin accounts and injected a gambling-spam script into the site's files. The flood wasn't the attack; it was the cover. The real damage was happening at the access level.

None of this was in the scope of work. It landed mid-week, with no warning.

The Response

Detection to containment: two hours. The first priority was scoping the real blast radius — not the surface DDoS traffic, but whether the site was compromised at the access level. It was. All four unauthorized admin accounts were found and removed, the injected script was identified and cleaned across every affected file, and the entry point was traced and closed.

Hardening: five more hours. Once the immediate threat was neutralized, the deeper work started — making sure the same attack couldn't happen twice. Traffic filtering and rate limiting were configured at the edge, code-editing paths were locked down at the platform level, every legitimate credential was rotated, and active monitoring was put in place — with follow-up checks in the days after to confirm no residual access remained.

The more significant outcome wasn't the cleanup — it's that the site came out of the incident with layered security it didn't have before the attack. The breach made the platform stronger than it was the day before it happened.
